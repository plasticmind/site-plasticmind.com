**I have a confession to make.**





I hate loud, growling motorcycles.  No, no.  I don&#8217;t think you understand; I&#8217;m not saying that I think they&#8217;re tacky or silly or just not my style.  I&#8217;m saying that when those bulky Harley-Davidson motorcycles gurgle by my house at midnight with their obscene roar, rattling every window pane in the neighborhood, waking small children and causing dogs to bark, I want to shoot their riders off.  I do.  I jump up to the window like a maniac and glare at them, storing their faces away for the day that I snap.





Those motorcycles make me instantaneously hate the people driving them.  Oh, they&#8217;re probably nice people.  But when they open the throttle and bellow past my house, it&#8217;s like someone ripping open my windows, climbing through and shouting at the top of their lungs in my face that they&#8217;ve got money.  **&laquo;PUNCH&raquo;**  





Now don&#8217;t pull the whole, &#8220;it&#8217;s a safety feature&#8221; argument.  That&#8217;s a load right there.  There are plenty of other safety features that don&#8217;t involve coming into my house and pulverizing my tympanic membrane.





So if you&#8217;ve got a offensively loud motorcycle and you happen to be near my house, you&#8217;ve been warned.  Same thing goes for you bass-so-loud-it-makes-grandma-cry people.  (Jesse, that really doesn&#8217;t sound like a confessional.  Oh yeah?  **&laquo;PUNCH&raquo;**)
