Here beneath the fleeting sky, orange and close,<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My coffee-spoon existence flies<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through the darkness, down a stream of shimmer grey.<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The yellow line, now on, now off, beats my waiting heart&mdash;<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The one in my throat.





Battles on the island mind wax and wane,<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With strangers dressed to play the part<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of paraclyte, conscience and the hostage held.<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The hallway clock, now tick, now tock, strikes the fatal cut,<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thrusts the time-worn blade.





Beyond I hear the sirens wail, phantoms cry,<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As deep within emotions fail<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And morning's certain tastes like midnight's tear.<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sun is set, now gray, now black, shadows fill the eve<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Save the smoking flax.
