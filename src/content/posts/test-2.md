After a good deal of thought, prayer and godly counsel, Jessica and I have decided that we're going to begin the process of moving back to Upstate New York.

Let me start by saying that we’re not leaving because we've been asked to. In fact, Pastor and Robin tried to convince us to stay when we told them about this decision.  I'm also not leaving because I love the church any less or because I've lost my desire to minister.  Some of our life's greatest moments have happened right here at this church, and God has blessed us tremendously here.

It’s precisely because of the great love in our hearts for the people of OVC that we agonized over this decision.  Since the first day we arrived here, Pastor and Robin showed us love and support and have treated us like family—as have many of you.  But there are several reasons we decided now:

</div>
</div>

<p style="color: #222222;">First, God has been burdening my heart about a change in the shape of my ministry. Jess and I still plan to be involved in ministry—it’s an integral part of who we are—though we don’t know exactly what that will look like right now.  I’ve been feeling more of a burden to write and to minister in ways that just aren’t possible in my current role.



<p style="color: #222222;">There are also some practical considerations that went into this decision:



<div class="im" style="color: #500050;">

As many of you know, our family has outgrown our house—2 bedroom and 3 kids, and if God is willing, more to come. We initially considered adding on to our house, but after it became obvious that wasn't feasible (for a number of reasons), we decided that a new house was the best option. We had been discussing the possibility of moving closer to our parents for a while, and now felt like the right time for such a move.

Our kids are young and a move at this age will be easier than, say, when they're teenagers.

Another factor in our decision was the cost of living in this area. This is especially relevant for two important reasons:

First, most of the work that I do is mobile, I can do it from virtually anywhere. That makes staying in the area difficult, especially as we consider a new house.

Second, as I've shared with some of you, my work situation is in flux right now. I should find out by year's end whether or not I have continued employment. And losing this job would make getting approved for a house much more difficult.

God-willing, we're planning to put our house on the market some time in mid-September, so it may be several months before we move.

Practically speaking, we have several people who will be stepping up to help fill in the various roles left in my absence. Obviously, a musician is one of the greatest needs to be filled, and Pastor and I have been working to find someone that can fill that role.

This was not an easy decision. It's one we've been praying over for a while now.  When we first started attending OVC in 2005, Jessica was finishing up her degree at PBU and we didn't have any long-term plans to stay in the area.  God obviously had different plans, and we ended up staying, serving and growing in ways we never could have imagined. OVC is where our children—and some of our most meaningful friendships—were born.

</div>

<p style="color: #222222;">But God is moving us on, like Abraham. As I mentioned before, we're still planning to be involved in some kind of ministry, though I'll probably take some time to pray and think about what's next before jumping back too quickly into pattern of ministry.



<div class="im" style="color: #500050;">

*“I don’t know how you say goodbye to whom and what you love. I don’t know a painless way to do it, don’t know the words to capture a heart so full and a longing so intense.” —Laura Wiess*

</div>

<p style="color: #222222;">We can truly say that we have been changed for the better by our time here, and I pray that you can say the same about our time with you.  Thank you, from the bottom of my heart, thank you, for the trust you've placed in me and my family and for the great love and care you've shown us in our time here.
