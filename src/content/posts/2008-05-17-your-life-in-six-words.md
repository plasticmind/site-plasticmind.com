Inspired by Hemmingway's powerful six-word short story (“For sale: baby shoes, never worn.”) and SmithMag's *Six Word Memoirs*, I ran <a href="https://twitter.com/plasticmind/statuses/812820380">a contest</a> to see who could give me the most original and clever six-word autobiography.





The story was limited to six words, but the topics were all over the place. Some people gave a strictly autobiographical breakdown of their lives:





**Burton Andrews** &raquo; Internet and Tae Kwon Do addict. <br />
**Bud Gibson** &raquo; Lived all over the world. Teach. <br />
**Joe D'Andrea** &raqu0; Born. Played. Learned. Worked. Loved. Living. <br />
**Aaron O'Dell** &raquo; Born, Raised, Loved, Lost, Surviving Reborn.  





Other people focused on specific parts of their lives or personalities:





**Matt Marshall** &raquo; You're only normal because I'm crazy. <br />
**Mark Norwood** &raquo; Child four, let's hope no more. <br />
**Nathan Gardner** &raquo; Intense anticipation, followed by daily jubilation.  





Spiritual truths were another shared theme:





**Renee Doiron** &raquo; Complication inspires fear, yet Faith conquers. <br />
**Katie Hostetter** &raquo; One passion, one vision, one voice. <br />
**Angela Prince** &raquo; Born in sin, raised in Christ. <br />
**Tim Challies** &raquo; Born dead: redeemed. Reborn: alive. <br />
**Matt Gridley** &raquo; When tired of religion, chose Christ. 





Several people expressed angst:





**Melanie Aroniss** &raquo; I don't have a life story. <br />
**Cody Loveland** &raquo; Always in trouble for stupid things. <br />
**Bryan Collick** &raquo; A treadmill: building stamina, going nowhere. <br />
**Rob Kenny** &raquo; Waiting to find out the punchline.





While others still were painfully self-aware:





**Jeremy Killian** &raquo; I am compensating for something inadequate. <br />
**Angela Prince** &raquo; Madly in love, scared of loss. <br />
**Becky Stanton** &raquo; Becky, you need to lose weight. <br />
**Kristina Ferrone** &raquo; Always leaving the better things unsaid.  





But the award for the most original and clever autobiography goes to Becky for her frank and hilarious entry.





**Becky Stanton** &raquo; Born naked and hungry. Better now.  





The <a href="https://www.cafepress.com/plasticmind.265255594">t-shirt</a> is all yours, Becky!  If you email me your address and the shirt size you'd like, I'll send it out ASAP.





I also have to give Andy Klimek an honorable mention for his submission which made me laugh out loud:





**Andy Klimek** &raquo; I poop parties and raise eyebrows.
