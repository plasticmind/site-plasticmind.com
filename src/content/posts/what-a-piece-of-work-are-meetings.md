Meetings seem to suck the life out of me.





I was in meetings for about three and a half hours today, and I&#8217;m sitting here on the train, exhausted.  I&#8217;m not just emotionally drained, I&#8217;m physically tired and all I did most of the day was sit and listen to someone present ideas.  Don&#8217;t get me wrong, most of the meetings I was in were interesting and thought provoking; I was looking at some fairly cutting-edge web technology.





But here I am, like the boy from the Gary Larson *Far Side* cartoon asking to be excused because my brain is full.





Why?





I&#8217;m sure part of it physical: sitting for hours on end makes anybody lethargic.  Maybe we should all start lobbying for treadmills in our conference rooms.  Better yet, let&#8217;s do our conferences outside while we&#8217;re all cycling around town.





But I think another part of the &#8220;meeting drain&#8221; is psychological.  We spend so much time talking about all the great things we&#8217;re going to accomplish that we never have enough time to **actually** accomplish them.  We often leave our meetings with an excitement that quickly fades away once we realize it&#8217;s almost time to go home.





I know meetings are important.  Or rather, the reasons we often have them is important: everyone needs to be on the same page; everyone needs to stay accountable to other team members; everyone needs to catch a glimpse of what&#8217;s next.   These are very important goals&#8212;especially in large organizations where failure in these areas usually means a project trainwreck&#8212;that often need face-to-face interaction to accomplish





But as soon as meetings become detached from practical action&#8212;actually doing stuff&#8212;creativity withers and dies.  &#8220;And thus the native hue of resolution is sicklied oe&#8217;r with the pale cast of thought, and enterprises of great pith and moment with this regard their currents turn awry, and lose the name of action.&#8221;
